FCI*

The neutral level of financial conditions consistent with output at potential. FCI* is the financial-conditions analog of r*: insulated from financial market fluctuations, it provides real-time guidance on monetary policy stance.

Last updated: —

Financial Conditions Gap (FCI − FCI*)

The gap between observed financial conditions and their neutral level. A positive reading means financial conditions are tighter than neutral — restraining GDP growth. A negative reading means conditions are looser than neutral — boosting GDP growth. Units are percentage points of next-year GDP growth: a gap of +1 implies financial conditions are on track to reduce GDP growth by approximately 1 percentage point relative to neutral.

FCI and FCI*

FCI* (our estimate) and the underlying FCI series it is based on. FCI measures the tightening impulse of recent financial conditions on GDP growth: higher values mean tighter conditions, lower values mean looser conditions. FCI* is the neutral level consistent with output at potential. The FCI series shown is the FCI-G baseline index (3-year lookback) from Ajello et al. (2023).

Output Gap

One-sided (real-time) estimate of the output gap — GDP relative to potential, in percent. Positive values indicate an overheating economy; negative values indicate slack.
